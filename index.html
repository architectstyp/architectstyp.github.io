Every joule of energy put into these buildings is meaningful and offset.
We study the Whole Site Life Cycle Assessment to make our buildings efficient, from start to forever.

We need to build better, now. The building sector consumes nearly half of all energy produced in the United States, while 23% of new homes have defective windows and doors, 21% have roof issues, and 18% have inadequate framing. Tend your planet, typ.




We pledge to design every home:

⩘ Lean

Net zero energy use and net zero carbon footprint must be affordable. We use more effective and efficient methods to build your home than anything else out there. A high-performance home reduces energy, pollution, and dollar costs.

Buildings and construction account for more than 35% of global final energy use and nearly 40% of energy-related CO2e emissions.

We assess carbon footprint using a Whole Site Life Cycle Assessment, which includes every foot of travel and every step of production, use, and re-use. We then balance the equation of your site + construction + your home to design a carbon-neutral lifetime footprint. And, because solar panels can now pay for themselves after only two years, your zero-utility-bill home will start paying back, fast.

⩄ Resilient

New homes must be long-lasting and well-detailed. Our structural design uses less wood than traditional framing, and offers more long-term flexibility. Our envelope is warmer, drier, and protects the home more completely than any other assembly in use. Construction uses energy, no matter what. We get the most out of what we put in.

It would take 437 Ponderosa pines over 50 years to offset the cumulative 460 metric ton CO2e footprint of just one average Washington home.

The average new 2,400 sf home has a direct embodied carbon footprint of over 43 metric tons of CO2e emissions. It will consume 8.35 metric tons of CO2e per year, totaling a footprint of over 460 metric tons CO2e emissions over its 50 year lifespan.

That is why our homes are built to last 3x to 4x longer than typical construction. Think 200 years. We design to the most innovative and rigorous construction practices. Standard material dimensions reduce construction waste. Local materials contain less embodied carbon. Rainwater is aggressively collected, stored, and reused on-site. Our exterior and interior walls contain the fewest and most highly efficient layers possible. Our structural system allows the exterior shell to be completely independent from the interior walls, which also means that your home’s 200 year lifespan can easily have many iterations.

⋇ Healthy

Every home must be free of toxins and pollutants. Inadequate ventilation is responsible for 52% of sick building syndrome cases. We bring in plenty of fresh air. We offer healthier materials and finishes, both interior and exterior. Our homes are free from the chemical and biological contaminants of typical construction, such as formaldehyde and other VOCs, combustion byproducts, and mold.

The EPA found levels of about a dozen common organic pollutants to be 2 to 5 times higher inside homes than outside, regardless of whether the homes were located in rural or highly industrial areas.

To ensure clean indoor air quality, all of our building materials are certified by Declare labels and are free from the Living Future Red List. We use formaldehyde-free and low/no-VOC products throughout the home, whether it’s something you’ll touch everyday or never see. Proper ventilation expels chemical and biological buildup, both for your health and your home’s health. In the world of sustainable building techniques, there is an important balance between airtightness for energy performance and ventilation for a healthy building. We understand these factors and how to tune the building for optimum health.

We meet and exceed the standards set by the globally recognized leader in sustainable building, the International Living Future Institute. Every building we build stands up to their two measures: Zero Energy certification and Zero Carbon certification. We also meet and exceed the criteria for the US Department of Energy’s Zero Energy Ready homes, and the US Environmental Protection Agency’s Energy Star Homes and Renewable Energy Ready Homes. We participate in the EPA’s Indoor airPLUS program, utilize Watersense products, and perform HERS rating tests to ensure a thermally airtight home.

We are meeting the 2030 Challenge, today.
